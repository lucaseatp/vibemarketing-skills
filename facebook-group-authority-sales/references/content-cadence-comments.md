# Content, Cadence, and Comments

Use this reference to create posts and replies after checking the interview, rules, key people, and history.

## Initial Sequence

Create 3 initial pieces of content per group:

- Post 1: general engagement. Community question, local recommendation, or light conversation. No selling.
- Post 2: belonging with soft CTA. Real story connected to the group and a soft CTA, if allowed.
- Post 3: useful authority. At least 3 weeks after the second post. Practical content that shows real experience.

Publish post 2 one to two weeks after post 1. After post 2, keep at least 3 weeks between new posts in the same group.

## Formats

- Prioritize text.
- In post 1, an image is not necessary. A Facebook text post over a background is fine.
- Starting with post 2, use an image when it proves real experience, local presence, or humanity.
- Avoid generic ad-like images.
- Avoid aggressive sales creative.

## CTAs

Prefer:

- Phone.
- Website.
- DM.

Avoid:

- Scheduling link as the default.
- Form as the default.
- Long or pushy CTA.

## Post Pattern

1. Native hook: sound like a group member.
2. Specific local detail.
3. Human context.
4. Value, gratitude, or usefulness.
5. Light CTA, if allowed.

## Agnostic Example

Post 1:

```text
Does anyone have a recommendation for [local place/activity/resource] in [area]? I want to get to know the local options better and figured this group would have good suggestions.
```

Post 2:

```text
I went to/checked out/tried [group recommendation] after a few people here suggested it. Great recommendation. [True personal detail]. I work with [segment] in the area and like understanding the community I serve. If anyone ever needs an honest opinion on [problem the user solves], feel free to reach me at [allowed phone/website/DM].
```

Post 3:

```text
Three signs that [common audience problem] needs attention now, and three signs it can wait.
```

## Comments

Always reply to every comment on the user's posts. Use short, human, friendly replies. Do not force a sale in casual comments.

Do not enter third-party posts asking for a direct provider recommendation when the thread is already full of self-promotion. The goal is not to compete for attention with other sellers, but to build natural authority.

For someone asking for help:

```text
Happy to take a look. What part of town are you in? Did this come up recently, or has it been happening for a while?
```

For someone asking price too early:

```text
It depends on the specifics and what is actually going on. If you send a couple of details or reach me at [phone/website/DM], I can tell you whether it looks urgent before you spend money on anything.
```

For an admin concern:

```text
Totally understand. I am here to be useful to the group, not to spam it. I can edit or remove the contact line if that fits the rules better.
```
