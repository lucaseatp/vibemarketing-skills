# Key People and Partnerships

Use this reference to identify who is already influential in the group and how that person can make the user's entrance into the community easier.

## Who To Look For

- Admins and moderators.
- Members who answer many questions.
- People frequently tagged as recommendations.
- Owners of complementary local businesses.
- Event organizers.
- Community leaders, volunteers, coaches, association members, or resident leaders.
- People who publish posts with high engagement.

## What To Observe

- What topics these people start.
- What language they use.
- What type of post gets positive comments.
- Who comments frequently.
- Which pains appear repeatedly.
- Which businesses or services are well received.
- Which offers create rejection.

## How To Use In The Strategy

Suggest:

- Content that connects with topics these people already bring up.
- Helpful comments on their posts, without pitching.
- Polite direct contact when there is a legitimate reason.
- Local partnerships: event, referral exchange, educational content, volunteer initiative, sponsorship, live session, or collaborative post.
- Context requests: asking an admin whether a specific type of post is allowed before publishing.

## Direct Approach

Use direct contact only when there is context and clear value. Example:

```text
Hi [name]. I saw that you organize a lot of the conversation in the group around [topic]. I work with [service] in the area and wanted to better understand how I can contribute without breaking the group rules. Is there any kind of educational content that tends to be useful here?
```

Avoid cold mass messages, direct pitches, or asking for favors without a relationship.

## Record

Record key people in the group file at `groups/<group-slug>.md` with:

- Name.
- Role in the group.
- Profile URL or note, if available.
- Topics they move.
- Possible content bridge.
- Possible partnership.
- Risk or care needed in the approach.
