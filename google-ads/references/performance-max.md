# Performance Max (PMax)

## What it is

Google’s automated campaign that serves across Search, YouTube, Gmail, Discover, Display, Maps, and other inventory. You supply an **asset group** (copy, images, videos, logo) and **audience signals** — not rigid targeting. The algorithm optimizes for conversions recorded in the account.

## When to use

| Scenario | Recommendation |
| --- | --- |
| New account, **low** tier (&lt; US$ 200/day) or little history | **No** — start on Search (or Shopping if ecommerce with mature feed) |
| Lead gen, local service, launch, human sales follow-up | **After** Search (and ideally Demand Gen + remarketing) is stable |
| Ecommerce / direct site sale with purchase tag | **May test** early, with CPA or ROAS cap and healthy Merchant Center feed |
| ~30–50 primary conversions / 30 days and reliable tracking | **Test** PMax as **complement**, not Search replacement |
| Need volume scale with stable CPA | PMax often scales better than Search (less violent budget jumps) — only if lead/sale is **qualified** in CRM |

Typical order for **conversion/lead** (not ecommerce):

```text
1. Search (own search intent)
2. Demand Gen (prospecting with audience control)
3. Remarketing (when list exists)
4. PMax last (scale + complement)
```

PMax is **complementary**: on active search queries, Search usually takes priority over PMax.

## When to avoid or pause

- Thin budget split across many campaign types.
- Primary conversion = lead and **no** CRM/survey to validate quality.
- “Cheap” CPA in Ads but poor close rate (young leads, Display, weak intent).
- Maximize conversions **without** CPA cap in lead gen (spends on bad volume).
- Customer list under ~100 contacts for signals (weak lookalike signal).

## Why lead gen struggles on PMax

1. **No audience control** — optimizes form converters, not buyers later (more signups from broad/young traffic).
2. **Display network** in the mix — cheap clicks, weak leads; direct sale filters this, lead gen does not.
3. **Wrong metric** — low CPL ≠ business; compare Search/Demand Gen **and** CRM qualification rate.

## Setup that matters

### Conversions and bidding

- Primary: **sale** or **qualified lead** (not micro: scroll, pageview).
- Lead gen: prefer **target CPA** (cap), not open “maximize conversions.”
- Ecommerce: target CPA or **target ROAS** per margin.
- Wait for learning (~30–50 conversions in campaign or 2–3 weeks) before judging.

### Asset group

- Fill fully: multiple headlines, descriptions, image formats, short direct videos.
- Creative is the main lever — invest; test angles (speed, proof, offer, objection).
- Study competitors and **differentiate** copy (do not repeat generic AI RSA).

### Audience signals (suggestions, not targeting)

Hot signal priority:

1. **Buyer** / customer list (ideal ≥100 valid contacts)
2. Remarketing: checkout, key pages, YouTube subscribers
3. Up to **25 search terms** (what ICP actually searches)
4. Demographics/interests as **suggestions** only

At creation: configure **new customer acquisition** (bid more for new vs heavy remarketing).

### Landing page (lead gen)

- Message match with creatives.
- For PMax leads: LP **more direct and higher friction** than warm traffic (less polish, more filter) often improves quality.
- **Qualifying form + conditional conversion**: fire tag only when ICP criteria met (e.g. age, profile, region) — algorithm learns only on leads you want repeated.
- Duplicate LP for PMax only when testing qualification.

### Reporting

- Review **search terms** and **channel insights** (Display vs Search vs YouTube).
- Match PMax leads to CRM within 24–48h in early spend days.
- Do not scale because CPL dropped; scale when **qualified leads** close.

## Budget scaling

- On **very high** tier (≥ US$ 20k/month), large PMax budget jumps sometimes **do not** spike CPA like Search — still validate CRM quality before scaling.
- Seasonal lead gen (launch): pause at cycle end; restart requires re-warming.

## Quick checklist before launching PMax

- [ ] Search (or core) already converts with verified tracking
- [ ] CPA/ROAS cap defined
- [ ] Complete, differentiated creatives
- [ ] Hot signals (list + search terms)
- [ ] Aligned LP; lead gen qualification plan (form or conditional conversion)
- [ ] Process to measure quality outside Ads (CRM, calls, sales)
