## Page job

One page = one primary conversion. Every section supports a single next step.

## Above the fold (5-second test)

The reader must answer:

1. What is this?
2. Who is it for?
3. What do I get?
4. Why believe it?
5. What do I do now?

Template:

```text
Headline: primary outcome or value hook
Subheadline: for whom + how / constraint
Primary CTA: specific action (not "Submit")
Trust line: proof, guarantee, or qualifier (only if true)
```

## Section order (default)

1. Hero — promise or value hook + CTA
2. Problem — reader's world in their words
3. Solution — what you do differently (not a feature dump)
4. Benefits — outcomes in bullets (3–7)
5. Proof — testimonials, logos, numbers, demo (confirmed only)
6. How it works — 3 steps max
7. Objections — FAQ or risk reduction
8. Offer + CTA — repeat primary action
9. Trust footer — privacy, terms, contact if needed

Skip sections that don't move conversion. Short pages often beat long ones on high-intent traffic.

## Scannability

- Subheads every 2–4 short paragraphs
- Bullets instead of text blocks
- Bold **at most one phrase per block** — not whole paragraphs
- One CTA style repeated; secondary CTA only if the action is truly different

## Message match

- The hero should mirror the ad, email, or search intent that brought the visitor.
- If the ad taught "5 tax mistakes", the hero continues that thread — don't swap in a generic brand slogan.

## Checklist

- [ ] One primary outcome stated clearly
- [ ] Reader segment obvious on first screen
- [ ] Benefits outweigh features
- [ ] Every claim has proof or is marked `[PROOF NEEDED]`
- [ ] Objections handled before final CTA
- [ ] Specific CTA verbs ("Book 15-min call", not just "Learn more")
- [ ] No hype or unverifiable guarantees
