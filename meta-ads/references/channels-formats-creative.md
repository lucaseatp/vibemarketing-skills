## Feed vs Reels vs Stories

| Placement | Typical use | Watch for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Feed** | Broad reach, static and video, remarketing | Higher CPM on competitive niches; fatigue on same creative |
| **Reels** | Top/mid funnel, hook-heavy video, younger audiences | Needs native vertical video; weak static underperforms |
| **Stories** | Urgency, swipe-up, short offers, retargeting | Low dwell time; strong hook in first 1–2s |

**Recommendation:**

- Discovery: test **Reels + Feed** with same message, different format (1-1-3 per placement or Advantage+ placements).
- Scale: double down on placement with best CPA/ROAS; don't force Reels if Feed wins on same creative concept.
- Remarketing: Feed + Stories often work; Reels optional for refresh.

## Image vs Video

| Format | When | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Static image** | Clear offer, proof, before/after, low production | Saturation faster; needs frequent refresh |
| **Short video (15–60s)** | Demo, testimonial, UGC, SaaS screen capture | Higher production cost; winner lasts longer |
| **Carousel** | Multiple benefits, steps, social proof slides | Complex message; good for consideration |

Rules:

- Same **angle** can run as image and video — test both in 1-1-3.
- SaaS: **screen demo + voiceover** often beats generic lifestyle (see `saas-meta-ads.md`).
- Low ticket info products: strong **static + headline** can outperform over-produced video.

## WhatsApp vs Landing Page

| Destination | When | Tracking |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Landing page** | Scalable funnel, pixel events, A/B test LP | Full funnel metrics (CTR → LPV → CVR) |
| **WhatsApp / Messenger** | High-touch sale, local service, low digital maturity | Often **no** reliable purchase event — don't optimize cost cap on "engagement" |
| **Instant form** | Lead gen, B2B, quick qualification | Lead event; quality depends on form fields |

**Recommendation:**

- E-commerce, SaaS, info with checkout → **LP + purchase/lead event**.
- Local/clinic/high ticket with human close → WhatsApp ok for **conversation**, but measure offline or use qualified lead event.
- Don't mix **engagement campaigns** (comments, WhatsApp opens) with **sales** KPI in same report without labeling.

## Engagement vs Conversion

- **Engagement** (likes, comments, video views): cheap reach, warms audience — **not** a substitute for conversion optimization at scale.
- **Conversion / Sales**: optimize for purchase, lead, complete registration — use for cost cap and ROAS decisions.
- Warm audiences from engagement can feed **remarketing** — refresh every ~30 days (`scale-ecosystem.md`).

## Creative Volume and Fatigue

Signals:

- Frequency ↑, CTR ↓, CPM ↑, CPA ↑ on same ad.
- One creative carrying >70% of account spend.

Actions:

- Maintain **3–5 active winners** in ecosystem (multiple 1-1-1).
- Parallel **1-1-3** pipeline; duplicate campaign to test, never inject into selling campaign.
- Rotate hooks every 2–4 weeks in competitive niches; SaaS can run winners longer if demo is specific.

## Value-First Creative (Cross-Skill)

For scripts, hooks, and "value ad" structure (problem → insight → proof → CTA):

→ `copywriting` skill → `references/value-ad-creative.md`

Meta-specific application:

- **Hook in first 3s** (Reels/video) or **headline on static** — same rule as value creative.
- **One clear CTA** per ad; match LP message.
- **Social proof** in creative reduces LP bounce.

## Channel Decision Rules

- CTR bottleneck → test **format/placement** (Reels vs Feed, video vs static) before new audiences.
- CVR bottleneck with good CTR → fix **LP/offer**, not only creative.
- Local/high-touch → WhatsApp may win; still track **qualified lead** or offline sale.
- Scale account with one format → add **second format** same angle before 2× budget.
