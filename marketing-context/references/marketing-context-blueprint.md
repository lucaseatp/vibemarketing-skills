# Marketing Context Blueprint

Use this blueprint to create or restructure `marketing-context.md`. Adapt section depth to the maturity of the business and the amount of source material. Do not include sections that add clutter without helping future marketing work.

The user may have no formal docs. In that case, still create the file from conversation, rough notes, website/app context, and clearly labeled assumptions.

## Depth Guidelines

Use a short version when the business is early or the user needs speed:

- Business Snapshot
- Offer
- ICP
- Problems and desired outcomes
- Positioning
- Proof
- Brand voice
- Channels and CTAs
- Assumptions and open questions

Use the full version when the file will serve multiple downstream skills or a team.

Use a discovery-first version when there are no docs:

- Business Snapshot
- Offer And Monetization
- Ideal Customer Profile
- Customer Problems, Desires, And Language
- Positioning And Value Proposition
- Proof And Claims
- Brand Voice
- Channels And Campaign Context
- Constraints And Guardrails
- Assumptions And Open Questions

It is acceptable for early versions to contain assumptions and open questions. It is not acceptable to leave the file as a hollow template.

## Writing Standards

- Use clear prose and practical bullets.
- Prefer specific claims over generic statements.
- Keep tables for proof, assumptions, objections, pricing, and source inventory.
- Preserve the user's terminology when it is clear and useful.
- Mark unknowns instead of filling gaps with generic marketing language.
- Keep examples tied to the user's actual business.
- Avoid internal process notes that do not help future marketing execution.
